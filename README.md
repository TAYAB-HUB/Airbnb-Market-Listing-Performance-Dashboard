# Airbnb_Market_Listing_Performance_Dashboard
1. Project Title / Headline
🏠 Airbnb Market & Listing Performance Dashboard
An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze Airbnb’s listing growth, market share, accommodation pricing, city-level ratings, and customer review behavior across major global cities.
2. Short Description / Purpose
The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of Airbnb’s market performance across 279,712 listings, 182,024 hosts, and 10 cities. It helps users understand historical growth, city competitiveness, pricing patterns, customer satisfaction, and seasonal travel trends.
3. Tech Stack
The dashboard was developed using:
📊 Power BI Desktop – Dashboard development and visualization.
📂 Power Query – Data cleaning, transformation, and preparation.
🧠 DAX – KPI calculations, percentages, cumulative measures, and dynamic analysis.
🗂️ Data Modeling – Connecting listings, hosts, cities, property types, ratings, and reviews.
📁 File Formats – .pbix for development and .pdf/.png for presentation.
4. Data Source
Source: Airbnb listings and reviews dataset.
The dataset contains information on:
279,712 listings
182,024 hosts
187,999 reviews
144 property types
10 major international cities
Room types and average prices
Host and Superhost status
Customer ratings and review activity
Listing creation dates and monthly review trends
5. Features / Highlights
Business Problem
Airbnb operates across highly competitive tourism markets, making it difficult to quickly compare listing supply, pricing, customer satisfaction, and travel demand across cities.
Important questions include:
Which cities have the largest market share?
How has Airbnb’s listing growth changed over time?
Which property types command the highest prices?
Which cities receive the strongest customer ratings?
How frequently do customers leave reviews?
How does review activity change throughout the year?
Goal of the Dashboard
The dashboard aims to:
Monitor Airbnb’s historical listing growth.
Compare market share across major cities.
Analyze Superhost and non-Superhost listings.
Compare average prices by room type.
Evaluate customer ratings across key service dimensions.
Identify review frequency and seasonal travel patterns.
Support data-driven pricing, marketing, and expansion decisions.
Walkthrough of Key Visuals
Key KPIs
Displays 279,712 listings, 10 cities, 182,024 hosts, 144 property types, and 187,999 reviews.

New Listings Trend
Tracks listing growth from 2008 to 2021 across private rooms, entire places, shared rooms, and hotel rooms. New listings peaked in 2015, slowed during 2016–2017, recovered before 2020, and declined sharply during COVID-19.

Market Share by City
Compares Superhost and non-Superhost listings across cities. Paris, New York, and Sydney together represent almost half of total listings and 48% of reviews.

Average Price by Room Type
Hotel rooms have the highest average price at $800, followed by entire places at $673, shared rooms at $580, and private rooms at $462.

City Ratings Matrix
Compares accuracy, communication, cleanliness, location, and value ratings. Mexico City records consistently strong ratings across all categories.

Review Frequency Analysis
Shows how often customers leave reviews. Approximately 98.8% of customers submitted three reviews or fewer, while one unusual customer recorded 283 reviews.

Monthly Review Distribution
Highlights seasonal demand. Paris and Rome dominate review activity from April to August, while New York experiences stronger activity in November and December.

Business Impact & Insights
Market Prioritization: Identify cities with the strongest listing supply and customer activity.
Pricing Strategy: Compare prices across room types to support competitive pricing.
Customer Experience: Use rating categories to identify service strengths and improvement opportunities.
Seasonal Marketing: Plan campaigns according to city-specific travel seasons.
Host Performance: Compare Superhost penetration across markets.
Data Quality Monitoring: Detect unusual activity, such as exceptionally high review counts.
Strategic Expansion: Discover cities with lower Airbnb penetration and potential growth opportunities.
